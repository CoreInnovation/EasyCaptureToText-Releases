## What it does

| Hotkey | Action |
|--------|--------|
| **Ctrl + Alt + C** | **Capture region → text.** The screen freezes, you drag a box over any text — a PDF that won't let you select, a video frame, an error dialog — and it lands on your clipboard as plain text. Paste it anywhere. |
| **Ctrl + Alt + T** | **Clipboard image → text.** Already have an image on your clipboard (a Win+Shift+S snip, a copied picture)? This reads its text and **pastes it straight into whatever you're typing in.** |

Both hotkeys are rebindable in Settings. The app lives in your system tray and starts with Windows.

## Why it's different

- ✅ **Offline & private** — OCR runs on your PC using the engine built into Windows. Your captures **never leave your device.**
- ⚡ **Instant** — no screenshot → upload → "generating…" round-trip. It's a reflex.
- 🪄 **The paste-from-image trick** — clipboard image to typed text in one keystroke.
- 🫥 **Tiny & out of the way** — tray app, starts with Windows, forget it's there.

## Install

1. [Download `EasyCaptureToText.exe`](https://github.com/CoreInnovation/EasyCaptureToText-Releases/releases/latest/download/EasyCaptureToText.exe)
2. Run it. That's it — it tucks into your system tray (teal capture-frame icon) and shows a quick welcome.
3. Press **Ctrl+Alt+C** and drag over some text. Paste. 🎉

> Windows SmartScreen may warn on first run (new unsigned app). Click **More info → Run anyway**.
