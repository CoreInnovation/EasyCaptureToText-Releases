Two hotkeys do everything

Ctrl + Alt + C

Capture region → text

The screen freezes, you drag a Snipping-Tool-style box over any text — a PDF you can't select, a video frame, an error dialog, a paywalled page — and it lands on your clipboard as plain text.

Ctrl + Alt + T

Clipboard image → text

Already have an image on your clipboard (a Win+Shift+S snip, a copied picture)? This reads its text and pastes it straight into whatever you're typing in. Nothing else does this.

How it works

  1. Install & forget. It tucks into your system tray and starts with Windows.
  2. Press the hotkey. Drag a box over text, or point it at a clipboard image.
  3. Paste. The text is on your clipboard — drop it anywhere. Done.

Questions

Is it really free?

Yes. Download and use it.

Does it work offline?

Completely. It uses the OCR engine built into Windows — no internet, no account, no data leaves your PC.

What do I need?

Windows 10 or 11. That's it — the download bundles everything else.

Where does my captured text go?

Only to your clipboard. Your captured text and images are never sent anywhere.

Does it connect to the internet at all?

Only to check for updates — an anonymous ping you can switch off in Settings. It never carries anything you captured.
